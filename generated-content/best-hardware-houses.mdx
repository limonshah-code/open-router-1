## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Hardware Houses

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations

Before diving into specific products, clearly define where and how you'll use the hardware. For ceiling fixtures, measure your room's ceiling height and existing electrical box placement. Flag brackets require knowing your flagpole diameter and wall material (wood, brick, stucco each need different anchors). Garage door hardware must match your door's style—carriage house accents work best on traditional panel doors. Consider whether you need single pieces or bulk packs. A homeowner doing one bathroom upgrade has different needs than a landlord renovating multiple units. Matching hardware to your specific application prevents costly mistakes and returns.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications

Different hardware types have different critical features. For lighting: check bulb type compatibility (A19, candelabra, LED integrated), wattage limits, and dimmer compatibility. Note whether bulbs are included. For hinges: determine bearing type (plain vs ball-bearing), pin type (removable vs fixed), and corner radius (square vs 5/8" or 1/4" radius). Ensure the hinge size matches your door thickness. For flag brackets: verify inner diameter matches your pole (most are 1" but some are 1.5"), check weight capacity, and note mounting hardware included. For garage hardware: measure your door panel dimensions and choose hinges/handles that span appropriately. The mounting method (screw-on vs clamp-on) matters for your door material. Always check if the product is rated for your environment—bathroom fixtures need damp-location ratings, exterior hardware needs weather resistance.
