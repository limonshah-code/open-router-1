## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Cordless String Trimmers Under 100

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before picking a trimmer, assess your specific yard conditions. Measure the area you need to maintain—small urban lawns under 1,000 sq ft can often be handled by lightweight 20V models, while larger suburban lots may benefit from higher voltage or dual batteries. Consider the type of vegetation: thin grass requires less power than thick weeds or light brush. Think about storage space; compact models with folding handles fit in small sheds. Finally, decide if you need edging capability—many 2-in-1 designs offer both, but dedicated edgers may outperform convertibles for heavy border work.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
While our focus is under 100, prices within this range vary. Truly budget-friendly corded models sit below 60, offering basic power without batteries. Entry-level cordless kits (tool + battery + charger) typically run 70–90. Premium all-in-one systems with multiple batteries and accessories can approach or slightly exceed 100 but often provide better long-term value. Factor in hidden costs like extra batteries (20–40 each) or replacement spools. Setting a clear budget helps narrow options, but remember that spending a bit more often gains meaningful improvements in runtime, power, or durability.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Prioritize features that match your needs: voltage (20V for light jobs, 40V for tougher weeds), battery capacity (Ah—higher means longer runtime), cutting width (10–14 inch—wider covers ground faster), line diameter (0.065 inch standard, thicker for dense brush), and feed system (auto-feed is convenient, bump-feed may be more reliable in heavy use). Speed control (variable or 2-speed) helps balance power and battery conservation. A tilting head aids slope work, and a spacer guard protects plants. For edging, wheeled guides create straighter lines than fixed-guard models.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Check build materials—metal gear housings and reinforced shafts last longer than all-plastic components. Look for sealed bearings and weather-resistant battery contacts if you’ll store the tool outdoors. Weight distribution matters; a well-balanced tool reduces fatigue. Warranty length indicates manufacturer confidence; 2–3 years is common in this range, with some brands offering longer. Read user reviews focusing on long-term durability, especially around the string head, gearbox, and battery contacts. A tool that feels solid and sturdy in hand often outlives a lighter, cheaper-feeling counterpart.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Sticking with known brands like Worx, Black+Decker, Greenworks, and WeGofly generally ensures better customer support, readily available replacement parts (batteries, chargers, spools), and consistent product quality. These brands invest in R&D for battery technology and motor efficiency. Off-brand models may save money initially but often suffer from poor battery life, weak motors, or lack of service networks. Check if the brand has a broad ecosystem—compatible batteries across multiple tools add future value and reduce total cost of ownership.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
If you have limited garage or shed space, look for compact designs with telescoping shafts or foldable handles. Weight is crucial for carrying up and down stairs or for extended use—models under 5 lbs are notably easier to manage. Cordless tools inherently offer better portability than corded ones, but heavier batteries increase weight. Consider how you’ll store the tool; a wall-mount charging cradle can save floor space. Also, think about transport to a community garden or rental property—lighter, cordless models are far more convenient.

Purchasing a cordless string trimmer under 100 is a smart investment for most homeowners with average yard maintenance needs. Within this budget, you can find capable 20V models that handle routine grass and weeds, or even a 40V option for more demanding tasks. Over time, the cost per use is low, especially if you already own other tools in the same battery ecosystem, allowing you to share batteries across multiple devices. While they may not match the runtime or peak power of top-tier gas models, the convenience, ease of storage, and environmental friendliness make cordless trimmers under 100 a wise choice for the vast majority of residential users.
