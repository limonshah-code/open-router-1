### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Look for solid materials like aluminum—it resists rust better than steel or iron. Powder-coated finishes last longer than painted ones. Glass should be thick enough to withstand impacts. Check warranty length—better brands offer 1-5 year warranties. Read reviews for long-term durability reports, especially in coastal or harsh winter climates. Quality construction pays off with years of trouble-free service, while cheap fixtures may corrode or fail within seasons.

When investing in permanent outdoor fixtures, sticking with reputable brands ensures reliability and saves money from premature replacements.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
Fixture dimensions determine placement—measure your space before buying. Post mounts need sufficient post height and clearance. Chain hung lanterns require ceiling height and sturdy mounting. Consider weight for installation—heavy fixtures need reinforced mounting and two people. Smaller fixtures suit tight spaces like beside doors or on narrow walls. Larger statement pieces need expansive walls or large porch ceilings. Think about future access—will you need to reach bulbs for replacement? Integrated LEDs eliminate this concern.
